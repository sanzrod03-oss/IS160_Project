          Tips for Accurate Results

          Follow these guidelines to get the most accurate disease detection results

          1

          Use Supported Plants Only

          The AI is trained on 8 specific crops: Apple, Grape, Orange, Peach, Potato, Squash, Strawberry, and Tomato. Using other plants will result in incorrect predictions.

          2

          Good Lighting is Essential

          Take photos in natural daylight or bright, even artificial lighting. Avoid shadows, backlighting, and dark conditions that can obscure leaf details.

          3

          Focus on Affected Leaves

          Capture clear, close-up images of leaves showing symptoms. The leaf should fill most of the frame. Avoid blurry or distant shots.

          4

          Check Multiple Predictions

          Always review the top 3 predictions and their confidence scores. If the top prediction has low confidence, consider the alternatives.

          5

          Consult Professionals

          This tool is a guide, not a replacement for expert advice. For important decisions, always consult local agricultural extension services or certified plant pathologists.

          6

          Understand AI Limitations

          Even with 99.9% accuracy on test data, AI can make mistakes with unusual cases, poor image quality, or plants outside its training data.
